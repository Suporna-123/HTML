Discover the universe: Learn about the history of the cosmos, what it's made of, and so much more.

QPEs, or quasi-periodic eruptions, are a class of X-ray flares made when objects move through gas disks surrounding supermassive black holes. Studying QPEs can help us understand how these monster black holes interact with their environments — places where gravity, energy, and matter behave in ways we can’t replicate on Earth. Using data from telescopes, scientists have probed one of these mysterious phenomena, bringing us a step closer to cracking their cosmic rhythm.
